Page 1 - Battle of Britain (1940)

The Battle of Britain was the first major military campaign fought entirely in the air. The Royal Air Force successfully defended Britain from German air attacks. It made the Luftwaffe's might slowly starting to bleed.

Importance

  • Stopped Germany from invading Britain.
  • Boosted Allied morale.
  • Marked Germany's first major defeat.
  • Germany's casualty rate for the battle are; 2,500 to 2,662 aircew killed and 1,700 aircraft lost

Page 2 - Battle of Midway (1942)

The Battle of Midway was one of the most decisive naval battles of World War II. The United States destroyed four Japanese aircraft carriers.

Importance

  • Turned the tide of war in the Pacific.
  • Reduced Japan's naval strength.
  • Allowed Allied forces to begin advancing.

Page 3 - D-Day (June 6, 1944)

More than 150,000 Allied soldiers landed on the beaches of Normandy, France. It was the largest amphibious invasion in history.

Importance

  • Opened a Western Front.
  • Started the liberation of Western Europe.
  • Weakened German defenses.

Page 4 - Liberation of Paris (1944)

The Allies, together with the French Resistance, liberated Paris from German occupation. The city was freed on August 25, 1944.

Importance

  • Restored French independence.
  • Boosted Allied morale.
  • Forced German forces to retreat.

Page 5 - Final Allied Victory (1945)

Germany surrendered on May 8, 1945 (Victory in Europe Day). Japan surrendered on September 2, 1945, ending World War II.

Importance

  • Ended World War II.
  • Freed occupied countries.
  • Led to the establishment of the United Nations.

Summary

The Allies achieved victory through cooperation, determination, and major military successes. These triumphs changed world history and restored peace after years of conflict.
